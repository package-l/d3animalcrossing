What are we looking at?

Per its Wikipedia page, Animal Crossing: New Horizons grossed $654 million in global digital sales by the end of 2020, making it the year's seventh highest-grossing digital premium game worldwide. As of March 31, 2021, the game has sold 32.63 million units. Because of the timing of the release around the start of the pandemic, the game not only was popular in terms of sales, but it allowed for a very interactive community of people stuck in their homes to come together from all over the world and help each other out in building great islands.

With the data the team obtained from scraping the tweets made by people commenting on the Animal Crossing New Horizons 2.0 update, I created a visualization to track the opinions and topics that people are discussing through their emoji usage!

So what does the Twitter community for Animal Crossing look like since the update?

As you can see, the Animal Crossing community is still very active on social media platforms. As you may have gleaned from some of the visualizations above, there seemed to be a lot of activity starting on November 3rd and November 4th, prior to the stated release date. Well, that is because it dropped early! According to Polygon and many of the Twitter users as well, the release surprisingly came out about a day earlier than expected. Did you notice it in your explorations?

By tracing the activity of the Animal Crossing community on Twitter, it seems apparent to our team that fans of the game are both excited and very happy about the update! The engagement fans have with each other about the game is extremely lively and overwhelmingly positive. We challenge you to find a single tweet with negative sentiment. Our team thinks it's a great time to pick up the game, start playing, and join the community!

Data Sources and Methods

All our source codes, datasets, data preprocessing scripts, and images can be found in our GitHub repo.

    Data
    • The data used in our visualizations are filtered from 7 days of tweets containing the keyword "AnimalCrossing" extracted via Twitter's Standard Search API. We preprocessed and analyzed the data in order to provide you great visualizations and insights.

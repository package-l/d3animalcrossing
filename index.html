What are we looking at?

Originally part of a team project, our team was analyzing the Twitter activity around the time of the Animal Crossing New Horizons 2.0 update. For clarity, this website was created to highlight my visualization and contribution to the team project. For more information on the team's work, scroll to the bottom!

I created a visualization to help analyze the opinions and topics the Animal Crossing community is discussing on Twitter by looking at their emoji usage!

Data Sources and Methods

All our source codes, datasets, data preprocessing scripts, and images can be found in our GitHub repo.

    Data
    • The data used in our visualizations are filtered from 7 days of tweets containing the keyword "AnimalCrossing" extracted via Twitter's Standard Search API. We preprocessed and analyzed the data in order to provide you great visualizations and insights.
    Additional inspiration
    • Importantly we also gathered inspiration for our text bubbles from here.
    Images
    • None of the images on this webpage are ours; the images were collected from Google image search, and respective source links are commented. Additional icons were found from the Animal Crossing Wiki.

    Instructions

    Click the Categorize button to see the emojis split into their respective categories and get a sense for which types of emojis were most popular. You can merge them back as well to see an overall comparison of what emojis the Animal Crossing community liked to use the most!

    Hover over each emoji to see how many times it was used over the week of November 4th and to see what other emojis it was tweeted with! Notice how the emojis that have not been used with the selected emoji will fade into the background.
